Pearl introduces us to notation for potential outcomes: Y_{X=x}(U) represents the outcome person U would have if they also had X = x. We can simplify this to Y_x

The implication is Y_x is just as real as Y even though we might not be able to observe Y_x for a given person &amp; x.
