Just occurred to me: Is't the front-door a simple example of getting an unbiased and stable estimator of Y, given observations on X and Z (with U varying)? 
[ X---&gt;Z---&gt;Y  with confounder U, X&lt;-U-&gt;Y. ]
E[Y|x,z] is unstable, whereas the front-door formula is.
