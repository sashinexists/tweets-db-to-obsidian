@y2silence I interpret Horst's surprise (upon finding a suppressor in the data, 1941) as evidence that regression
analysts expect correlations to behave like separation in graphs, that is, if a node Y is separated from X and from Z, it must also be separated from the pair (X,Z).  #Bookofwhy
