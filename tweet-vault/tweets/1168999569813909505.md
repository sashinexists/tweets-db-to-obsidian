@yudapearl X is head(1)/tail(0) for a tossed coin. Y is head/tail for another coin. S is a child of X and Y (if X=Y, then S=1; otherwise, S=0). Here, S is uncorrelated with X and Y. X and Y are independent, so knowing X alone is useless. But if I know both X and S, I perfectly predict Y.
